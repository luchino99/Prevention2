Privacy notice — Uelfy Clinical

Last updated: 2026-05-04 · Version: 1.0

This page describes how Uelfy Clinical (the “Platform”) processes personal data on behalf of healthcare providers (“Controllers”) who use the Platform for cardio-nephro-metabolic risk assessment and longitudinal patient monitoring. It is a technical privacy notice intended for transparency to clinicians and end users.

This document is not legal advice. The Controller remains responsible for the lawfulness of processing under Articles 6, 9, 13 and 14 GDPR and must publish its own privacy notice tailored to its data subjects. Uelfy operates as a Processor under Article 28 GDPR governed by a per-tenant Data Processing Agreement.

1. Roles under GDPR

Controller. Each Tenant (clinic, hospital, independent practitioner) determines the purposes and means of processing for the patient data it enters into the Platform.

Processor. Uelfy operates the technical infrastructure on behalf of the Controller under a written DPA (Article 28 §3 GDPR). Sub-processors are listed in §11.

2. Categories of personal data processed

  • Identity / contact: full name, email, professional role of clinicians; pseudonymous patient reference (UUID + tenant-supplied external code), patient first/last name (when entered by the clinician), date of birth or birth year, biological sex.
  • Special-category health data (Article 9): vital measurements, lipid panel, glycaemia, kidney function markers, liver function markers, anthropometrics, lifestyle questionnaire answers, validated risk scores, clinician-entered notes, imaging report references.
  • Authentication telemetry: hashed IP address, truncated user-agent, MFA factor verification timestamp, access timestamps for audit purposes.
  • Operational metadata: tenant identifier, professional-patient link assignments, consent records, audit events, retention policy values.

3. Purposes and lawful bases

Each Tenant configures its own lawful basis. Typical mappings for European clinical Controllers:

  • Provision of clinical risk assessment and follow-up: Article 6(1)(b) (contract with the patient or clinician), with Article 9(2)(h) (provision of healthcare) for special-category data.
  • Audit and security logging: Article 6(1)(c) (legal obligation) and Article 6(1)(f) (legitimate interest of the Controller in ensuring traceability).
  • Optional AI commentary (off by default; opt-in only): Article 6(1)(a) explicit consent and Article 9(2)(a) for special-category data. Disabled in Tenants where the DPA does not include the OpenAI sub-processor addendum.

4. Data minimisation

Uelfy stores only the lab values, vitals and lifestyle answers required to compute the validated scores listed in §10. The Platform does not track marketing analytics, behavioural events outside the audit trail, third-party advertising identifiers, or session recordings. Free-text fields are limited to clinician notes scoped to a single patient record.

5. Retention

Default retention windows can be tightened or extended by each Tenant in their Tenant Settings page (subject to the DPA):

  • Active patient records: kept while the patient is clinically active; soft-deleted on revoke or discharge.
  • Soft-deleted patients: 30-day grace period (configurable), then irreversible anonymisation by the daily cron.
  • Audit events: 10 years (configurable 30 days … 10 years).
  • Resolved alerts: 1 year (configurable 7 days … 5 years).
  • Notification queue: 90 days (configurable 7 days … 1 year).
  • Clinical PDF report storage: 2 years (configurable 90 days … 10 years; database row metadata is kept for traceability after the storage object expires).
  • DSR records: 10 years (Article 12(3) record-keeping).

6. Data subject rights

Patients exercise their rights through the Controller. The Platform supports the Controller in fulfilling them:

  • Article 15 (access): machine-readable JSON export of every record we hold for a patient.
  • Article 16 (rectification): in-app edit of patient demographics and clinical context.
  • Article 17 (erasure): soft-delete with grace period, then anonymisation that strips all PII while preserving deterministic score outputs for epidemiology (legal basis Article 6(1)(f) with DPIA).
  • Article 18 (restriction): temporary suspension via patient inactivation flag.
  • Article 20 (portability): same JSON envelope as Article 15.
  • Article 21 (objection): handled controller-side per the published lawful basis.
  • Article 7(3) (withdrawal of consent): consent revocation creates a new immutable consent record with granted=false, audited via strict-mode.

7. Security of processing (Article 32)

  • Encryption in transit (TLS 1.3) on every endpoint.
  • Encryption at rest (Supabase platform-managed AES-256; per-tenant KMS envelope on roadmap).
  • Mandatory multi-factor authentication for administrative roles; per-role flags for clinician / staff cohorts.
  • Row-level security with FORCE on every PHI table.
  • Strict audit guarantees on consent, deletion, export, report generation, and DSR transitions.
  • Distributed rate limiting (Upstash Redis) to resist abuse.
  • Content Security Policy with no inline scripts and no third-party CDNs in the clinical UI.
  • Annual restore drill SOP; PITR with ≤ 5 min RPO.

8. Pseudonymisation

Patient identifiers in URL paths and audit logs are UUIDs, never names or emails. The patient pseudonymous reference is the Tenant-supplied external code where present, otherwise the synthetic UUID. Audit IP addresses are SHA-256 truncated to 32 characters before persistence.

9. Cross-border data transfers

By default, all Platform data is stored and processed in the European Union (Vercel EU region, Supabase EU region, Upstash EU region). Non-EU sub-processors (e.g. OpenAI for the optional AI commentary feature) are activated only with the Controller's explicit opt-in via DPA addendum, with Standard Contractual Clauses and a transfer impact assessment on file.

10. Children's data

The Platform's clinical scores (SCORE2, SCORE2-Diabetes, ADA, FIB-4, eGFR CKD-EPI 2021, ATP III metabolic syndrome, FRAIL, FLI, BMI, PREDIMED, Mifflin-St Jeor) are validated for adults aged 18 and over. The intended use excludes paediatric populations.

11. Sub-processors

  • Vercel Inc. — application hosting (region: EU Frankfurt).
  • Supabase Inc. — database, authentication, object storage (region: EU Central / EU West).
  • Upstash Inc. — distributed rate-limit cache (region: EU West).
  • OpenAI L.L.C. — optional AI commentary (US; off by default; opt-in only via DPA addendum).

12. Contact

For security vulnerability disclosures: security@uelfy.com (RFC 9116 details at security.txt).
For data subject requests, please contact your healthcare provider (the Controller).

This notice is reviewed at least annually and on any material change to processing activities.
